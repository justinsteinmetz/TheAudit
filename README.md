# The Audit

A 20-minute classroom instrument disguised as a bucket list talk.

Students arrive having written personal bucket lists and expecting to discuss them. For five minutes, that's what happens. Then the deck renames itself, and the lists stop being work to be shared and become evidence to be examined: which of these desires did you originate, and which did you absorb?

**Live:** `https://justinsteinmetz.github.io/the-audit/`

## What's in the repo

| File | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| `index.html` | The projection deck. Single file, no dependencies beyond Google Fonts. Ten slides with built-in activity timers. |
| `lesson-plan.md` | Teacher-facing plan: slide-by-slide timing, verbal beats, facilitation notes, contingencies. |

## The arc

1. **Title** — the deliberately unfinished sentence
2. **Etymology** — *kick the bucket*; the genre is a response to mortality
3. **Why lists work** — one earnest beat, placed so it can be knocked over
4. **The question** — *But whose list did you write?*
5. **The reveal** — *This was never about bucket lists.* The deck renames itself: THE AUDIT
6. **Test 1 — The Postcard Test** (3:00) — tag every item that could appear on a travel poster or someone else's feed
7. **Test 2 — The Tomorrow Test** (3:00) — circle anything startable this week; impossible or just postponed?
8. **Test 3 — The Swap** (4:00) — exchange lists; defend the item that surprises your partner
9. **Harvest** — if you crossed out everything borrowed, what's left?
10. **Exit** — *How much of your future is currently being written by other people?*

No item is ever disqualified. The Postcard Test demands a defence, not a deletion — an item that survives the defence is more its owner's than it was before.

## Navigation

- **Advance:** → / space / tap right side of screen
- **Back:** ← / tap left quarter of screen
- **Timers:** click to start, click again to reset; they end on *discuss* and won't advance the slide
- Progress hairline along the bottom edge; slide counter bottom-left

Designed for projection (F11) but works on touch devices.

## Design

The house register: void background, Cormorant Garamond display, Inter body, grain overlay, single ember accent. The pacing is part of the instrument — one idea per slide, advanced by the teacher, with silence built into the sequence (slide 4 is intended to sit unspoken for several seconds).

## Pedagogy

A methodology exercise wearing a wellbeing topic. The transferable move — interrogate where a preference came from before treating it as yours — applies identically to ambitions, opinions, and media habits. The natural follow-up is running the Postcard Test against something other than bucket lists: career plans, or what students think they think about a current news story.

Built for a Year 9 guest segment; the format scales to any group old enough to have absorbed someone else's desires, which is every group.

## Deployment

GitHub Pages, root of `main`. Rename `the-audit.html` to `index.html` (or set Pages to serve from `/docs` and place it there). No build step.
